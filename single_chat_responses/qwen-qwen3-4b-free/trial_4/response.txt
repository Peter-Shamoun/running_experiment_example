  - **Mechanical Properties**:  
    - Young’s Modulus: 100 GPa  
    - Tensile Strength: 500 MPa  
    - Density: 1.5 g/cm³  
- **Dimensions**:  
  - **Shape**: Rectangular prism (stiffness optimization)  
  - **Length**: 30 cm (balance between rigidity and portability)  
  - **Width**: 10 cm (width for stability during recoil)  
  - **Thickness**: 1 cm (sufficient rigidity for energy transfer)  
- **Function**: Acts as a rigid base to absorb and distribute forces during launch.  

**B. Limbs (Elastic Storage Elements)**  
- **Material**: Carbon fiber-reinforced polymer (CFRP)  
  - **Mechanical Properties**:  
    - Young’s Modulus: 100 GPa  
    - Tensile Strength: 500 MPa  
    - Density: 1.5 g/cm³  
- **Dimensions**:  
  - **Length**: 20 cm (flexibility for energy storage)  
  - **Width**: 5 cm (width for lateral stability)  
  - **Thickness**: 0.5 cm (flexibility without excessive weight)  
- **Function**: Store elastic potential energy through deformation.  

**C. Trigger Mechanism (Release System)**  
- **Material**: Steel (high modulus for controlled release)  
  - **Mechanical Properties**:  
    - Young’s Modulus: 200 GPa  
    - Tensile Strength: 500 MPa  
    - Density: 8 g/cm³  
- **Dimensions**:  
  - **Spring Length**: 10 cm  
  - **Spring Diameter**: 0.5 cm  
  - **Spring Constant (k)**: 10,000 N/m (optimized for energy transfer)  
- **Function**: Releases stored energy via a spring-loaded mechanism, ensuring precise control.  

**D. Projectile (Ballistic Element)**  
- **Material**: Lead core with polymer casing  
  - **Mechanical Properties**:  
    - **Lead**: Density 11.3 g/cm³, Tensile Strength 15 MPa  
    - **Polymer Casing**: Density 1.2 g/cm³, Tensile Strength 50 MPa  
- **Dimensions**:  
  - **Length**: 5 cm (aerodynamic balance)  
  - **Diameter**: 2 cm (reduces air resistance)  
  - **Mass**: 100 g (optimized for range and accuracy)  
- **Function**: Transfers stored energy into kinetic energy, with minimal drag.

### **2. Energy Transfer Analysis**  
**A. Energy Storage in Limbs**  
- **Formula**:  
  $$
  E_{\text{limbs}} = \frac{1}{2} k_{\text{limb}} \cdot x^2
  $$  
- **Parameters**:  
  - $ k_{\text{limb}} = \frac{E_{\text{CFRP}} \cdot A}{L} = \frac{100 \times 10^9 \cdot (0.005 \times 0.005)}{0.2} = 1.25 \times 10^6 \, \text{N/m} $  
  - $ x = 0.1 \, \text{m} $ (maximum stretch)  
- **Result**:  
  $$
  E_{\text{limbs}} = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 1.25 \times 10^6 \cdot (0.1)^2 = 6250 \, \text{J} \quad (\text{Over-optimized for power})
  $$  
  *Note: Adjust $ x $ to 0.05 m for realistic energy storage (e.g., 1562.5 J).*

**B. Energy Storage in Trigger Mechanism**  
- **Formula**:  
  $$
  E_{\text{trigger}} = \frac{1}{2} k_{\text{trigger}} \cdot x^2
  $$  
- **Parameters**:  
  - $ k_{\text{trigger}} = 10,000 \, \text{N/m} $  
  - $ x = 0.05 \, \text{m} $  
- **Result**:  
  $$
  E_{\text{trigger}} = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 10,000 \cdot (0.05)^2 = 12.5 \, \text{J}
  $$  

**C. Energy Transfer Efficiency**  
- **Assumption**: 90% efficiency (minimal energy loss to frame and limbs).  
- **Total Energy Transfer**:  
  $$
  E_{\text{total}} = 0.9 \cdot (E_{\text{limbs}} + E_{\text{trigger}}) = 0.9 \cdot (1562.5 + 12.5) = 1400 \, \text{J}
  $$
