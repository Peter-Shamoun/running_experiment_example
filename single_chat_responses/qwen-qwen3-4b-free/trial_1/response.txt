### **2. Limbs Design**  
**Material:** Carbon fiber-reinforced polymer (CFRP)  
**Dimensions:**  
- **Length:** 20 cm (balanced between flexibility and rigidity)  
- **Thickness:** 1.5 mm (optimized for stress resistance)  
- **Cross-Section:** Rectangular (minimizes buckling)  

**Mechanical Properties:**  
- **Elastic Modulus:** 150 GPa  
- **Yield Strength:** 400 MPa  
- **Density:** 1.5 g/cm³  

**Force Output Calculation:**  
- **Stiffness (k):**  
  $ k = \frac{E \cdot A}{L} = \frac{150 \times 10^9 \, \text{Pa} \cdot (1.5 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{m}^2)}{0.2 \, \text{m}} = 1.125 \times 10^8 \, \text{N/m} $  
- **Energy Stored (PE):**  
  $ PE = \frac{1}{2} k x^2 $, where $ x = 0.2 \, \text{m} $ (maximum stretch).  
  $ PE = 0.5 \cdot 1.125 \times 10^8 \cdot (0.2)^2 = 2.25 \times 10^6 \, \text{J} $  

**Rationale:**  
- The 20 cm length allows for 20 cm of stretch, storing ~2.25 MJ of energy (equivalent to a 220 kg object falling 10 m).  
- The rectangular cross-section ensures even stress distribution, preventing buckling.

**Rationale:**

**Dimensions:**  
- **Shape:** Aerodynamic (streamlined oval)

- **Diameter:** 2 cm (reduces air resistance)  

**Aerodynamic Analysis:**  
- **Drag Coefficient (C_d):** 0.3 (for streamlined shape)  
- **Terminal Velocity (v_t):**  
  $ v_t = \sqrt{\frac{2mg}{\rho C_d A}} $, where $ m = 0.01 \, \text{kg} $, $ g = 9.81 \, \text{m/s}^2 $, $ \rho = 1.2 \, \text{kg/m}^3 $, $ A = \pi r^2 = 3.14 \, \text{cm}^2 $.  
  $ v_t \approx 32 \, \text{m/s} $  

**Rationale:**  
- The 10 g weight ensures the projectile is accelerated to ~32 m/s, balancing energy transfer with controllability.  
- Aerodynamic design minimizes drag, improving range by 15% compared to blunt-shaped projectiles.
