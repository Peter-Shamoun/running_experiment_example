### **2. Energy Transfer Analysis**  
**A. Elastic Potential Energy Storage**  
- **Formula**:  
  $$
  E = \frac{1}{2} \cdot E \cdot I \cdot \left(\frac{\Delta}{L}\right)^2
  $$  
  - **Variables**:  
    - $ E = 70 \, \text{GPa} $ (Young’s modulus of limbs)  
    - $ I = \frac{b h^3}{12} = \frac{0.02 \cdot (0.01)^3}{12} = 1.67 \times 10^{-12} \, \text{m}^4 $  
    - $ \Delta = 0.05 \, \text{m} $ (deflection)  
    - $ L = 0.5 \, \text{m} $ (limb length)  
- **Calculation**:  
  $$
  E = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 70 \times 10^9 \cdot 1.67 \times 10^{-12} \cdot \left(\frac{0.05}{0.5}\right)^2 = 0.125 \, \text{J}
  $$  

**B. Kinetic Energy Transfer to Projectile**  
- **Assumption**: 90% efficiency (accounting for friction and material deformation).  
- **Kinetic Energy**:  
  $$
  K = 0.9 \cdot 0.125 = 0.1125 \, \text{J}
  $$  
- **Projectile Velocity**:  
  $$
  v = \sqrt{\frac{2K}{m}} = \sqrt{\frac{2 \cdot 0.1125}{0.05}} = 2.12 \, \text{m/s}
  $$
