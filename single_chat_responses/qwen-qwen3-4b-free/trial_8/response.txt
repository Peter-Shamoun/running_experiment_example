**Comprehensive Slingshot Design Proposal**  
**Objective:** Construct a slingshot with optimized energy transfer, precision, and power, validated by quantitative analysis.  

---

### **1. Frame Design**  
**Material:** Carbon fiber-reinforced polymer (CFRP) composite.  
- **Dimensions:**  
  - **Length:** 60 cm (adjustable for energy storage).  
  - **Width:** 15 cm (for stability).  
  - **Thickness:** 10 mm (CFRP) for strength-to-weight ratio.  
- **Mechanical Properties:**  
  - **Tensile Strength:** 500 MPa.  
  - **Modulus of Elasticity:** 150 GPa.  
- **Rationale:** CFRP provides high stiffness and low weight, enabling efficient energy storage while minimizing material deformation. The 10 mm thickness ensures structural integrity under high tension.  

---

### **2. Limbs Design**  
**Material:** Carbon fiber-reinforced polymer (CFRP) for flexibility and strength.  
- **Dimensions:**  
  - **Length:** 30 cm per limb (symmetrical for balance).  
  - **Width:** 5 mm (CFRP) for flexibility.  
  - **Thickness:** 5 mm (CFRP) for tensile strength.  
- **Mechanical Properties:**  
  - **Elastic Modulus:** 150 GPa.  
  - **Yield Strength:** 500 MPa.  
- **Rationale:** The 5 mm thickness and 30 cm length allow the limbs to store energy efficiently. The CFRP material resists buckling under tension, ensuring consistent energy transfer.  

---

### **3. Trigger Mechanism**  
**Design:** Spring-loaded mechanism with a 10 cm long steel spring (4140 steel).  
- **Dimensions:**  
  - **Spring Length:** 10 cm.  
  - **Diameter:** 2 mm.  
- **Mechanical Properties:**  
  - **Spring Constant (k):** 150 N/cm (calculated via Hooke’s Law: F = kx).  
- **Force Output:**  
  - **Maximum Compression:** 5 cm (x = 0.05 m).  
  - **Force at Full Compression:** F = kx = 150 N/cm × 5 cm = **750 N**.  
- **Rationale:** The steel spring provides high stiffness, ensuring rapid energy release. The 5 cm compression maximizes stored energy (PE = 0.5 × k × x² = 0.5 × 150 N/cm × 0.05² = **18.75 J**).  

---

### **4. Projectile Design**  
**Material:** Polycarbonate (for aerodynamic balance).  
- **Dimensions:**  
  - **Diameter:** 2 cm (to fit slingshot’s opening).  
  - **Length:** 10 cm (for stability).  
- **Mass:** 0.15 kg (optimized for range/accuracy).  
- **Aerodynamic Properties:**  
  - **Drag Coefficient (Cd):** 0.3 (streamlined shape).  
- **Rationale:** The 2 cm diameter ensures minimal air resistance, while the 0.15 kg mass balances energy transfer and trajectory stability.  

---

### **5. Energy Transfer Analysis**  
- **Spring Energy Storage:**  
  - **PE = 0.5 × k × x² = 0.5 × 150 N/cm × (5 cm)² = 18.75 J**.  
- **Projectile Kinetic Energy:**  
  - **KE = 0.5 × m × v²**.  
  - Assuming 90% efficiency (energy loss due to friction), KE ≈ 16.88 J.  
  - **v = sqrt(2 × KE / m) = sqrt(2 × 16.88 / 0.15) ≈ 14.9 m/s**.  
- **Range Calculation (45° launch):**  
  - **R = (v² × sin(2θ)) / g = (14.9² × sin(90°)) / 9.81 ≈ 22.5 m**.
