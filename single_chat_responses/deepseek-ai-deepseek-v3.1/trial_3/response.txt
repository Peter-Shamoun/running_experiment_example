### **Projectile Propulsion System Design Proposal: 'The Trebuchet's Cousin'**

**Objective:** To design and construct a slingshot that maximizes kinetic energy transfer to the projectile (power) while minimizing dispersion of launch parameters (precision).

### **1. Frame Design: The Torque-Resistant Monocoque**

The frame's primary function is to provide an immovable anchor point for the bands, resisting the torque and bending moments induced during draw and release.

*   **Design:** A monolithic, asymmetrical "Y" frame with an integrated pistol grip and forearm brace.
*   **Rationale:** The monocoque structure eliminates joints, which are failure points for energy loss through flex. The asymmetry allows for a direct, linear pull along the arm's axis, aligning the force vector with the skeletal structure of the user for superior control and stability. The forearm brace distributes recoil forces over a larger area, reducing muscle fatigue and stabilizing aim.
*   **Material:** **6061-T6 Aluminum**. Chosen for its excellent strength-to-weight ratio and machinability.
    *   *Yield Strength (σ_y):* 276 MPa
    *   *Young's Modulus (E):* 68.9 GPa
    *   *Density (ρ):* 2.70 g/cm³
*   **Quantitative Analysis:**
    *   **Moment of Inertia (I):** The critical cross-section at the fork is designed with a **30mm x 20mm rectangular profile**.
        *   \( I = \frac{1}{12} \times b \times h^3 = \frac{1}{12} \times 0.02 \times (0.03)^3 = 4.5 \times 10^{-8} \, \text{m}^4 \)
    *   **Stress under Load:** Assuming a maximum draw force (`F_draw`) of 500 N (approx. 112 lbf) applied 0.2m from the fork, creating a bending moment (`M`).
        *   \( M = F \times d = 500 \, \text{N} \times 0.2 \, \text{m} = 100 \, \text{N·m} \)
        *   Bending Stress: \( \sigma = \frac{M \cdot y}{I} = \frac{100 \times 0.015}{4.5 \times 10^{-8}} = 33.3 \, \text{MPa} \)
    *   **Factor of Safety (FoS):** \( \text{FoS} = \frac{\sigma_y}{\sigma} = \frac{276 \, \text{MPa}}{33.3 \, \text{MPa}} = 8.3 \)
    *   **Conclusion:** The frame will experience negligible flex (δ), ensuring near-perfect energy transfer and return to a consistent neutral position, which is critical for precision.

### **2. Elastic Band System: The Tapered Latex Composite**

The bands are the energy storage medium. Efficiency is maximized by optimizing the taper ratio and material to ensure uniform strain energy distribution.

*   **Design:** A **tapered band** design, wide at the frame attachment and narrow at the pouch. This ensures that stress is evenly distributed along the entire length of the band upon elongation, preventing localized overstraining and failure. This design promotes a more uniform acceleration profile.
*   **Material:** **Layered Natural Latex Tubing (0.5" down to 0.25")**. Natural latex offers superior elasticity and energy return compared to synthetic rubbers.
    *   *Estimated Young's Modulus (E_latex):* ~0.01 - 0.1 GPa (highly variable, must be measured empirically for precise calculations).
    *   *Maximum Elongation:* >600%
*   **Quantitative Analysis:**
    *   **Active Length (L₀):** **0.25 m** (unstretched). This provides a good balance between power stroke length and practical draw length.
    *   **Taper Ratio:** 2:1 (0.5" to 0.25"). This ensures the entire band reaches its maximum safe strain simultaneously.
    *   **Draw Force Calculation:** Using Hooke's Law as a first approximation for a tapered band (where effective spring constant `k_eff` must be integrated).
        *   For a simplistic model of a straight band: \( k = \frac{E \cdot A}{L_0} \)
        *   Assuming `E` ~ 1 MPa, cross-sectional area `A` ~ 1.2e-4 m² (for 0.5" width, 1mm wall).
        *   \( k \approx \frac{1 \times 10^6 \, \text{Pa} \times 1.2 \times 10^{-4} \, \text{m}^2}{0.25 \, \text{m}} = 480 \, \text{N/m} \)
        *   For a **5x stretch** (draw length = 1.25 m), Force: \( F = k \cdot \Delta x = 480 \, \text{N/m} \times (1.25 - 0.25) \, \text{m} = 480 \, \text{N} \)
    *   **Stored Energy (U):** \( U = \frac{1}{2} k (\Delta x)^2 = \frac{1}{2} \times 480 \times (1.0)^2 = 240 \, \text{J} \)
    *   **Note:** A tapered band will have a lower `k_eff` and store more energy for the same peak force than a straight band. This is a key advantage, yielding higher power output.

### **3. Pouch and Release Mechanism: The Consistent Rolled-Hinge**

The pouch must hold the projectile securely and release it cleanly and identically every time.

*   **Design:** A rigid, molded **polycarbonate pouch** with a deep, conformal cradle and a rolled-hinge design.
*   **Material:** **Polycarbonate (Lexan)**.
    *   *Tensile Strength:* 55-75 MPa
    *   *High Impact Strength:* Prevents fracture from repeated stress.
*   **Rationale:** The deep cradle centers the projectile consistently. The "rolled-hinge" design ensures the pouch folds around the projectile during acceleration and peels away cleanly upon release with minimal interference, as opposed to a flapping pouch which can induce yaw. This is a critical feature for accuracy.

### **4. Projectile Design: The High-Sectional Density Sphere**

Projectile choice is critical for both range (aerodynamic efficiency) and energy transfer (minimizing energy loss to the band/pouch system).

*   **Design:** **Precision-grade 12.7mm (0.5 inch) diameter steel ball bearings (Grade 25)**.
*   **Material:** **AISI 52100 Steel** (Density: **ρ ≈ 7800 kg/m³**).
*   **Quantitative Analysis:**
    *   **Mass (m):** \( m = \rho \cdot V = 7800 \, \frac{\text{kg}}{\text{m}^3} \cdot \frac{4}{3}\pi (0.00635 \, \text{m})^3 = 8.35 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{kg} \, (8.35 \, \text{g}) \)
    *   **Sectional Density (SD):** \( SD = \frac{m}{\pi r^2} = \frac{0.00835 \, \text{kg}}{\pi (0.00635 \, \text{m})^2} \approx 65,700 \, \text{kg/m²} \)
    *   High sectional density is critical for penetrating air resistance efficiently, maximizing range.
    *   **Energy Transfer & Theoretical Muzzle Velocity (v):** Assuming 80% efficient transfer of stored elastic energy to kinetic energy (accounting for friction, hysteresis, and other losses), the estimated velocity is:
        *   \( KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 = 0.8 \times U \)
        *   \( v = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times 0.8 \times U}{m}} = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times 0.8 \times 240 \, \text{J}}{0.00835 \, \text{kg}}} \approx 214 \, \text{m/s} \)
    *   **Force Output (Impulse):** The average force on the projectile during launch is immense over the short acceleration time (`t_acc` ~ 0.01s).
        *   \( F_{avg} = \frac{\Delta p}{\Delta t} = \frac{m v}{t_{acc}} = \frac{0.00835 \, \text{kg} \times 214 \, \text{m/s}}{0.01 \, \text{s}} \approx 179 \, \text{N} \)

### **5. Integrated Performance Analysis**

*   **Power Metric - Muzzle Energy:** \( KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 = \frac{1}{2} \times 0.00835 \times (214)^2 \approx 191 \, \text{J} \)
    *   This is a significant energy output, comparable to a powerful air rifle, and should far exceed designs using weaker bands or lighter projectiles.
*   **Precision Analysis:** Precision is derived from consistent mechanics:
    1.  **Frame Stiffness (FoS=8.3):** Ensures zero shot-to-shot variation in anchor point geometry.
    2.  **Tapered Bands:** Promote even wear and consistent force-draw curve.
    3.  **Rolled-Hinge Pouch:** Provides a clean, repeatable release point.
    4.  **Spherical Projectile:** Eliminates aerodynamic instability (no lift or pitching moments) seen in non-spherical projectiles. The drag coefficient (`C_d`) for a sphere in this regime is ~0.47, providing predictable ballistic drop.
*   **Range Estimation (Neglecting Air Resistance):**
    *   \( R = \frac{v^2}{g} = \frac{(214)^2}{9.81} \approx 4670 \, \text{m} \)
    *   This is unrealistic due to drag. Using a more sophisticated model (e.g., CFD or iterative calculation), the actual range is expected to be several hundred meters, but the extreme initial KE demonstrates superior power.

### **Summary of Superiority**

This design outperforms competitors by fundamentally optimizing energy transfer at every stage:
1.  **Energy Storage:** Tapered bands store more energy per unit of peak force (higher efficiency).
2.  **Energy Transfer:** A rigid frame (negligible flex loss) and efficient pouch (clean release) ensure a high percentage (~80%) of stored energy becomes projectile KE.
3.  **Energy Application:** A high-sectional-density projectile retains its velocity better against air resistance than a lighter projectile, converting more of its KE into downrange energy.
4.  **Precision:** Every component is engineered for shot-to-shot consistency, minimizing the standard deviation of launch velocity and angle—the key to accuracy.
