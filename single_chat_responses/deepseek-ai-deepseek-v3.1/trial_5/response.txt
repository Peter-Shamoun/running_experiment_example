### **Project: 'Tensor' - A High-Performance Torque-Based Slingshot System**

**Design Philosophy:** The objective is to maximize the kinetic energy transferred to the projectile while maintaining a highly consistent launch vector. This is achieved not through raw force, but through optimizing the *efficiency* of the energy transfer from the user's pull to the projectile's release. The design prioritizes a linear force-draw profile, minimal energy loss to hysteresis, and a rigid frame to ensure shot-to-shot consistency.

---

### **1. Frame Design & Material Specification**

The frame's primary function is to provide an immutable anchor point for the bands, resisting torque and flex under full draw to ensure perfect alignment.

*   **Design:** **Dual-Triangle Monocoque Frame**. This design creates a rigid, triangulated structure that channels stress efficiently. The launch forks are integrated into the main body to eliminate any pivot points or screws that could introduce play. The handle is ergonomically shaped for a consistent, stable grip.
*   **Material:** **6061-T6 Aluminum** (or **Carbon Fiber Composite** if budget allows).
    *   **Why?** 6061-T6 offers an excellent strength-to-weight ratio (Tensile Yield Strength: **≥ 240 MPa**, Density: **2.7 g/cm³**). It is stiff, machinable, and resistant to fatigue. Carbon fiber (Tensile Modulus: **~230 GPa**) would be stiffer and lighter but more expensive and difficult to fabricate.
*   **Dimensions:**
    *   Overall Height (from base to fork tip): **280 mm**
    *   Fork Width (inner distance between forks): **45 mm** (Optimized for band attachment and minimizing "fork flip").
    *   Fork Tip Diameter: **12 mm** (to accommodate band wrap without sharp edges).
    *   Handle Grip Diameter: **30 mm** (optimized for a secure, comfortable hold).
*   **Analysis:** The frame's moment of inertia is calculated to resist the torque applied at full draw. Assuming a peak force (F_band) of 400 N applied 250 mm from the grip, the torque is **100 N⋅m**. The aluminum frame's cross-section is designed to keep deflection under this load to **< 0.5 mm**, ensuring negligible energy absorption by the frame and perfect alignment.

---

### **2. Elastic Band System (The "Engine")**

This is the core of the energy storage and transfer system. The goal is to maximize elastic energy storage while achieving a rapid, clean release.

*   **Design:** **Dual-Tapered Latex Tubes** (The "Flatband" design). Tapering reduces the mass at the pouch end, allowing for greater acceleration and a higher velocity for the same energy input. It also promotes a sequential, clean release from pouch to frame.
*   **Material:** **Natural Latex Rubber** (Professional-grade, 0.8mm wall thickness). Natural latex offers superior elasticity and a higher energy return ratio (resilience > 90%) compared to synthetic rubbers.
*   **Dimensions & Properties:**
    *   Unstretched Length (per side): **250 mm**
    *   Taper: **24 mm** (frame end) to **12 mm** (pouch end) flat width.
    *   Cross-sectional Area (avg.): **~19.2 mm²**
    *   Effective Spring Constant (k): Calculated from stress-strain data. For this latex grade, k is approximately **1800 N/m** *per band*.
    *   Maximum Strain (ε_max): Designed for **600%** (draw length 250mm → 1500mm stretched). This is within safe limits for high-quality latex to prevent premature failure.
*   **Force-Draw Analysis:** The force required increases linearly with draw length (Hooke's Law).
    *   F_draw = k_total * Δx
    *   k_total (for two bands) = 2 * 1800 N/m = **3600 N/m**
    *   At full draw (Δx = 1.5 m): F_draw = 3600 N/m * 1.5 m = **5400 N** (~540 kg force). *Note: This is the theoretical peak force. The user will not experience this fully due to the draw technique, but it defines the energy stored.*
*   **Energy Storage Calculation:**
    *   Elastic Potential Energy (E_elastic) = (1/2) * k_total * (Δx)²
    *   E_elastic = 0.5 * 3600 N/m * (1.5 m)² = **4050 Joules**
    *   This is the total energy input by the user. Not all is transferred to the projectile (see efficiency).

---

### **3. Pouch & Projectile Interface**

The pouch must release the projectile cleanly and consistently without imparting spin or drag.

*   **Design:** **Stiff Leather (or Synthetic Ballistic Nylon) Pouch** with precise, small finger holes.
*   **Material:** **1.5mm thick vegetable-tanned leather**. It is strong, has a high coefficient of friction to grip the projectile until release, and is stiff enough to prevent folding and snagging.
*   **Dimensions:** **40 mm x 60 mm** rectangle. Hole spacing optimized for a 12.7mm (0.5") caliber projectile.

---

### **4. Projectile Specification**

Projectile mass is critical in the energy-to-velocity conversion. Heavier projectiles carry more momentum but travel slower for the same energy. Lighter projectiles are faster but suffer more from drag.

*   **Chosen Projectile:** **12.7 mm (0.5 inch) Diameter Steel Ball Bearing** (Grade 25).
*   **Material:** **AISI 52100 Steel** (Density: **7850 kg/m³**).
*   **Mass Calculation:**
    *   Volume = (4/3)πr³ = (4/3)π(0.00635 m)³ = **1.07 x 10⁻⁶ m³**
    *   Mass (m) = Density * Volume = 7850 kg/m³ * 1.07e-6 m³ = **8.41 grams**
*   **Why?** This mass provides an excellent balance. It is heavy enough to retain kinetic energy over distance (high sectional density) and resist crosswinds, while being light enough to achieve high velocity. The spherical shape and smooth surface minimize aerodynamic drag (Drag Coefficient C_d ≈ 0.47).

---

### **5. Performance & Ballistics Analysis**

**A. Muzzle Velocity Estimation:**
Assume an energy transfer efficiency (η) of 80% due to hysteresis in the latex and other losses. This is a high but achievable efficiency for a well-designed tapered system.
*   Kinetic Energy (E_kinetic) = η * E_elastic = 0.8 * 4050 J = **3240 J**
*   E_kinetic = (1/2) * m * v²
*   Therefore, v = sqrt( (2 * E_kinetic) / m ) = sqrt( (2 * 3240 J) / 0.00841 kg ) )
*   **v ≈ 877 m/s** (This is a theoretical maximum under ideal conditions; real-world velocity will be lower due to dynamic losses, but it sets an upper bound for design comparison).

**B. Force Output:**
*   The **launch acceleration** is extreme. To reach 877 m/s in the approximate 0.5 ms it takes for the bands to unwind, acceleration (a) = Δv/Δt ≈ 877 / 0.0005 = **1,754,000 m/s²**.
*   The force on the projectile at launch is F = m * a = 0.00841 kg * 1,754,000 m/s² = **~14,750 N** (~1500 kg force).

**C. Range & Precision Analysis:**
*   **Range:** The theoretical maximum range (at a 45° launch angle in a vacuum) is R_max = v² / g ≈ (877²) / 9.81 ≈ **78,400 m**. In atmosphere, drag drastically reduces this. For a 12.7mm steel sphere, effective range for a target shot (flat trajectory) will be 100-200m, but the extreme velocity ensures a flatter trajectory and less drop, enhancing accuracy.
*   **Precision:** Precision is enabled by:
    1.  **Consistent Force Profile:** The linear "F vs. Δx" curve means the same draw length yields the same force every time.
    2.  **Rigid Frame:** Eliminates variable flex, ensuring the launch vector is identical shot-to-shot.
    3.  **Clean Release:** The tapered bands and stiff pouch prevent projectile yaw or spin induction.
    4.  **Aerodynamics:** The spherical projectile, while not perfect, has predictable and repeatable flight characteristics compared to irregular stones.
