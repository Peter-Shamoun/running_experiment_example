**Comprehensive Slingshot Design Proposal**

**Design Overview**

Our team presents a novel slingshot design, dubbed "Apex," engineered to maximize power and precision. The Apex slingshot leverages cutting-edge materials and innovative design elements to deliver exceptional performance in range and accuracy assessments.

**Components and Dimensions**

1. **Frame:**
	* Material: High-strength, lightweight carbon fiber (CFRP) with a Young's modulus of 350 GPa
	* Dimensions:
		+ Length: 300 mm (11.8 in)
		+ Width: 100 mm (3.9 in)
		+ Thickness: 5 mm (0.2 in)
2. **Pivoting Arm:**
	* Material: High-strength, lightweight aluminum alloy (6061-T6) with a Young's modulus of 70 GPa
	* Dimensions:
		+ Length: 150 mm (5.9 in)
		+ Width: 20 mm (0.8 in)
		+ Thickness: 3 mm (0.1 in)
3. **Sling:**
	* Material: High-strength, elastic rubber (e.g., EPDM) with a Young's modulus of 1.5 MPa
	* Dimensions:
		+ Length: 200 mm (7.9 in)
		+ Width: 20 mm (0.8 in)
		+ Thickness: 2 mm (0.08 in)
4. **Projectile:**
	* Material: High-strength, lightweight steel (AISI 4140) with a Young's modulus of 200 GPa
	* Dimensions:
		+ Diameter: 10 mm (0.4 in)
		+ Length: 50 mm (1.9 in)
		+ Mass: 10 g (0.35 oz)

**Design Features**

1. **Pivoting Arm Angle:** The pivoting arm is designed at a 30° angle to the horizontal, allowing for optimal energy transfer and reduced stress concentrations.
2. **Sling Tensioning System:** A precision-adjustable tensioning system enables users to fine-tune the sling's elasticity, ensuring consistent performance across various projectile weights.
3. **Projectile Rest:** A precision-machined, adjustable projectile rest ensures accurate alignment and consistent release.
4. **Grip:** Ergonomically designed grip with textured surfaces for secure handling and reduced fatigue.

**Quantitative Analysis**

**Energy Transfer and Range Assessment**

To evaluate the slingshot's performance, we'll use the following equations:

1. **Kinetic Energy (KE) of the Projectile:**

KE = (1/2) \* m \* v^2

where m is the projectile mass and v is its velocity.

2. **Range (R) of the Projectile:**

R = (v^2 \* sin(2θ)) / g

where θ is the angle of projection and g is the acceleration due to gravity.

**Force Output Analysis**

To estimate the force output, we'll use the following equation:

F = (E / L) \* A

where E is the energy stored in the sling, L is the length of the sling, and A is the cross-sectional area of the sling.
