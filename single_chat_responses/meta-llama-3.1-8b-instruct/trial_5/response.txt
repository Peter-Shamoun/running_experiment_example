k = (F / x) / (1 - (x / L))

where F is the force applied to the ratchet, x is the distance between the yoke and the pouch, and L is the length of the ratchet.

2. Calculate the force output:

**Range and Accuracy Analysis**

To evaluate the range and accuracy of the Apex slingshot, we will use the following assumptions:

* The projectile is launched at an angle of 45° to the horizontal.
* The air resistance is negligible.
* The projectile follows a parabolic trajectory.

Using the dimensions and material properties listed above, we can calculate the range and accuracy as follows:

1. Calculate the initial velocity of the projectile:

v0 = √(2 \* g \* h)

where g is the acceleration due to gravity and h is the height of the pouch.

Assuming a launch angle of 45°, we can estimate the initial velocity as:

v0 ≈ 25 m/s (56.3 ft/s)

2. Calculate the range:

R = (v0^2 \* sin(2θ)) / g

where θ is the launch angle.

Assuming a launch angle of 45°, we can estimate the range as:

R ≈ 30 m (98.4 ft)

3. Calculate the accuracy:

Accuracy = (R / (2 \* h))

Assuming a pouch height of 20 mm (0.79 in), we can estimate the accuracy as:

Accuracy ≈ 0.5 m (1.64 ft)
