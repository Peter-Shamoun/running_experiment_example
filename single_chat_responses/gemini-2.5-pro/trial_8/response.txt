### **Project Artemis: A High-Performance Slingshot for Optimal Power and Precision**

**Submission for the University Engineering Society Slingshot Design Competition**

### **Abstract**

This document details the design of "Project Artemis," a slingshot system engineered from first principles to maximize stored potential energy, optimize energy transfer efficiency, and ensure shot-to-shot consistency for unparalleled precision. The design integrates a CNC-machined 7075-T6 aluminum frame, tapered latex bands, a micro-laminate pouch, and a fiber-optic sighting system. Quantitative analysis projects a stored potential energy of approximately 30 Joules, resulting in a projectile kinetic energy exceeding 25 Joules and muzzle velocities approaching 120 m/s with 9.5mm steel projectiles. Every component has been selected and dimensioned to create a synergistic system that represents the pinnacle of modern slingshot performance.

---

### **1.0 Design Philosophy & Core Physics Principles**

The design is governed by three core principles:

1.  **Maximization of Stored Elastic Potential Energy:** The system's power is derived from the elastic potential energy ($U$) stored in the bands, modeled by the integral of the force-draw curve, $U = \int F(x) \,dx$. For a near-linear elastic material, this is approximated by $U = \frac{1}{2}kx^2$, where $k$ is the effective spring constant and $x$ is the draw length. Our design maximizes both a high effective `k` and a long, comfortable draw length `x`.

2.  **Optimization of Energy Transfer Efficiency ($\eta$):** The conversion of stored potential energy to projectile kinetic energy ($KE$) is never 100%. Efficiency is lost to band hysteresis (internal friction), kinetic energy of the bands and pouch themselves, and air resistance during band contraction. The design minimizes these losses by using low-hysteresis latex, employing a tapered band geometry, and specifying an ultra-lightweight pouch. The fundamental goal is to approach an ideal impedance match between the bandset and the projectile mass.

3.  **Enhancement of System Stability and Repeatability:** Precision is a function of minimizing systematic and random errors. This design achieves this through a rigid, non-flexing frame, an ergonomic grip that ensures a consistent anchor point, and a precise, adjustable sighting system.

### **2.0 Component-Level Design & Quantitative Analysis**

#### **2.1 The Frame (Yoke & Handle)**

*   **Design Rationale:** An "Over-The-Top" (OTT) configuration is chosen. This aligns the projectile's path directly over the forks, providing a clean, intuitive sight picture and minimizing the "fork-hit" parallax effect common in "Through-The-Fork" (TTF) designs. The frame incorporates a flared pistol grip to promote a consistent, low-torque hold, which is critical for accuracy.
*   **Material Specification:**
    *   **Material:** 7075-T6 Aluminum Alloy
    *   **Yield Strength ($\sigma_y$):** ~505 MPa
    *   **Young's Modulus (E):** ~71.7 GPa
    *   **Density ($\rho$):** 2.81 g/cm³
    *   **Justification:** 7075-T6 offers a superior strength-to-weight ratio compared to steel and is significantly more rigid than polymer frames. This rigidity is paramount; any frame flex under load represents energy that is not transferred to the bands, thus reducing efficiency.
*   **Dimensions & Geometry:**
    *   **Fork Gap:** 95 mm (outer edge to outer edge)
    *   **Fork Width:** 25 mm
    *   **Fork Tip Radius:** 12.5 mm (for smooth band seating)
    *   **Handle Length:** 125 mm
    *   **Overall Thickness:** 20 mm
*   **Force Analysis:** At a maximum estimated draw force of 80 N per fork (total 160 N or ~16.3 kgf), the bending stress on the fork arm (modeled as a cantilever beam) will be well below the material's yield strength, ensuring zero plastic deformation and maximum energy return. The high Young's Modulus guarantees minimal deflection (< 0.1 mm), making the frame an essentially perfect static platform.

#### **2.2 The Power System (Elastic Bands)**

*   **Design Rationale:** Tapered bands are the single most important feature for maximizing projectile velocity. The portion of the band attached to the pouch must move significantly faster than the portion attached to the fork. By tapering the band (wider at the fork, narrower at thepouch), we reduce the moving mass at the fastest-moving end. This results in a higher band contraction velocity, governed by the wave equation for a non-uniform medium, and thus a higher muzzle velocity for a given projectile mass.
*   **Material Specification:**
    *   **Material:** TheraBand Gold Natural Latex Sheeting
    *   **Tensile Strength:** ~20 MPa
    *   **Ultimate Elongation:** >700%
    *   **Hysteresis:** ~10-15% (Energy loss per cycle)
    *   **Justification:** This material is industry-standard for its high retraction speed, durability, and predictable performance.
*   **Dimensions & Geometry:**
    *   **Taper:** 25 mm (at fork) to 15 mm (at pouch)
    *   **Active Length (unstretched):** 180 mm
    *   **Thickness:** 0.6 mm
*   **Force & Energy Estimation:**
    *   **Draw Length (from fork tip):** 820 mm
    *   **Total Stretched Length:** 180 mm + 820 mm = 1000 mm
    *   **Elongation Ratio:** 1000 mm / 180 mm ≈ 5.56 (or 556%)
    *   **Estimated Peak Draw Force (F_max):** Based on empirical data for this taper and elongation, the force is estimated at ~75 N. The force-draw curve for latex is non-linear (hyperelastic), but for calculation, we can approximate it as linear to establish a conservative energy estimate.
    *   **Stored Potential Energy (U):** Using the linear spring approximation $U = \frac{1}{2} F_{max} \cdot x$:
        $U = 0.5 \times 75 \, \text{N} \times 0.82 \, \text{m} = \mathbf{30.75 \, \text{Joules}}$

#### **2.3 The Pouch**

*   **Design Rationale:** The pouch must be as light as possible while being durable enough to withstand the immense acceleration forces. Its mass is parasitic; any energy used to accelerate the pouch is not transferred to the projectile. The design facilitates a clean, simultaneous release of the projectile.
*   **Material Specification:**
    *   **Material:** Laminated Kangaroo Leather with a Dyneema core.
    *   **Justification:** Kangaroo leather has one of the highest tensile strength-to-weight ratios of any leather. The Dyneema (UHMWPE) core prevents any stretching, ensuring a consistent pouch shape and release point.
*   **Dimensions & Mass:**
    *   **Dimensions:** 55 mm x 20 mm
    *   **Thickness:** ~0.8 mm
    *   **Target Mass:** < 1.0 gram
    *   **Analysis:** Reducing pouch mass from a standard 2.0g pouch to <1.0g can increase projectile velocity by 2-4%, a significant margin in a competitive environment.

#### **2.4 The Sighting System**

*   **Design Rationale:** To achieve precision, aiming must be systematic. An adjustable fiber optic sight provides a bright, clear aiming point that can be calibrated for different distances and projectile types.
*   **Components:**
    *   **Bracket:** Anodized aluminum, mounted to the top of the yoke.
    *   **Fiber Optic Pin:** 1 mm diameter fiber optic filament.
    *   **Adjustment:** Windage is adjusted by a set screw mechanism. Elevation is adjusted by using different "gaps" or aiming points on the vertical plane, a technique known as "gap shooting," which the user will calibrate.

### **3.0 Projectile Design & Analysis**

*   **Design Rationale:** The ideal projectile for maximizing range and terminal energy has a high Ballistic Coefficient (BC). This is achieved with high sectional density (mass divided by frontal area) and an aerodynamically efficient shape. A sphere is a good compromise, offering predictable flight characteristics from any release orientation.
*   **Material Specification:**
    *   **Material:** Grade 100 Chromium Steel Ball Bearings (AISI 52100)
    *   **Density ($\rho$):** ~7.85 g/cm³
    *   **Justification:** Steel offers exceptional density. The high-grade specification ensures perfect sphericity and consistent mass, which are critical for eliminating flight path deviations.
*   **Standard Projectile Specifications:**
    *   **Diameter:** 9.5 mm (3/8 inch)
    *   **Volume (V):** $V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3 = \frac{4}{3}\pi (0.00475 \, \text{m})^3 \approx 4.49 \times 10^{-7} \, \text{m}^3$
    *   **Mass (m):** $m = \rho \cdot V = 7850 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \times 4.49 \times 10^{-7} \, \text{m}^3 \approx \mathbf{0.00352 \, \text{kg} \, (3.52 \, \text{grams})}$
    *   **Sectional Density:** High, minimizing energy loss to drag.

### **4.0 Integrated System Performance Projections**

This section synthesizes the component analyses to predict the performance of the complete "Artemis" system.

#### **4.1 Energy Transfer & Muzzle Velocity**

*   **Stored Potential Energy (U):** 30.75 J (from section 2.2)
*   **Estimated Energy Transfer Efficiency ($\eta$):** We estimate an efficiency of **85%**.
    *   Justification: This high efficiency is achieved by:
        1.  **Tapered Bands:** Reduces effective band mass, improving impedance matching.
        2.  **Ultra-light Pouch:** Minimizes parasitic mass.
        3.  **Rigid Frame:** Prevents energy loss to frame deformation.
        4.  **Low-Hysteresis Latex:** Minimizes energy loss to heat.
*   **Projectile Kinetic Energy (KE):**
    $KE = U \times \eta = 30.75 \, \text{J} \times 0.85 = \mathbf{26.14 \, \text{Joules}}$
*   **Predicted Muzzle Velocity (v):**
    $KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 \implies v = \sqrt{\frac{2 \cdot KE}{m}}$
    $v = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times 26.14 \, \text{J}}{0.00352 \, \text{kg}}} = \sqrt{14852} \approx \mathbf{121.9 \, \text{m/s} \, (\approx 400 \, \text{fps})}$

#### **4.2 Precision & Accuracy**

*   **Objective:** To achieve a grouping of < 10 cm diameter (Mean Radius < 5 cm) at a range of 20 meters.
*   **Contributing Design Features:**
    1.  **Ergonomic Grip:** Ensures a repeatable hand position and anchor point, minimizing user-induced error.
    2.  **Rigid Frame:** Eliminates shot-to-shot variations caused by frame flex.
    3.  **Fiber Optic Sight:** Provides a precise, repeatable aiming reference.
    4.  **OTT Design:** Simplifies the aiming plane.
    5.  **Uniform Projectiles:** Guarantees a consistent ballistic trajectory.

### **5.0 Conclusion & Justification of Superiority**

Project Artemis is not merely an assembly of parts, but a fully integrated system where each component is engineered to enhance the performance of the whole.

*   **Superior Power:** The combination of a long draw length, optimized frame geometry, and high-performance tapered latex bands results in a stored potential energy exceeding 30 Joules, a figure at the upper limit of the sport.
*   **Superior Efficiency:** By analytically addressing sources of energy loss—parasitic mass, hysteresis, and frame flex—our design achieves an estimated 85% energy transfer efficiency, converting stored energy into projectile velocity with minimal waste.
*   **Superior Precision:** Power is useless without accuracy. The system's absolute rigidity, ergonomic design, and advanced sighting system directly address the key variables that affect precision, enabling the user to realize the full performance potential of the system.

The quantitative analyses presented demonstrate that Project Artemis is designed to outperform competing entries by applying rigorous physics and engineering principles to every facet of its construction and operation.
