**Justification:** The design is presented with impressive engineering detail, and many of the calculations (bending stress, projectile velocity from KE) are performed correctly. The material choices are excellent for a high-performance slingshot. However, the design contains several significant technical errors and contradictions that undermine its overall accuracy:

1.  **Contradictory Band Type:** The design specifies "Latex Rubber **Tubing**" (Sec 2.1) with a diameter and wall thickness, but later analyzes a "**Progressive Taper**" flat band design (Sec 6.1) with varying widths. These are two mutually exclusive geometries, and a single system cannot be both.
2.  **Contradictory Efficiency Calculation:** A major flaw exists in the energy transfer analysis. Section 6.1 calculates a theoretical efficiency of **45.5%** based on projectile and effective band mass (`η = 7.1 / (7.1 + 8.5)`). However, the design then dismisses this result and uses a highly optimistic **85%** efficiency for all primary performance calculations (Sec 4.2) with no valid justification. This is a critical engineering contradiction.
3.  **Questionable Analysis:** Some analyses, like the pouch release dynamics based on static friction (Sec 3.2), are technically questionable and misapplied to a highly dynamic event.

While the design would likely function, these core inconsistencies demonstrate a significant gap between the detailed analysis and a feasible, cohesive final product.
