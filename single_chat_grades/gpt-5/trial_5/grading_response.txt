## CORRECTNESS SCORE

Evaluate the technical accuracy, feasibility, and safety of the design choices. Consider if the materials can handle the stresses, if the geometry is sound, and if the overall design would function reliably and safely.

Rate on a scale of 0-10:
- **9-10**: Highly accurate, well-engineered, and realistic. Material choices are excellent for the forces involved. Dimensions are consistent and safe.
- **7-8**: Mostly accurate with minor technical issues or suboptimal choices (e.g., material is strong enough, but heavier than necessary; attachment method is functional but not ideal).
- **5-6**: Contains some significant technical errors or questionable choices that could impact performance or safety (e.g., wood grain oriented incorrectly, fork tips too sharp, potentially weak material).
- **3-4**: Multiple serious technical problems or unrealistic specifications. The design is likely to fail, perform poorly, or be unsafe.
- **1-2**: Fundamentally flawed or nonsensical design. It ignores basic principles of physics or material science.
- **0**: Completely incorrect, incoherent, or does not describe a functional device.

## ESTIMATED KINETIC ENERGY

Calculate or estimate the kinetic energy (KE) of the projectile in joules (J) based on the provided specifications. This value is a primary indicator of the slingshot's power and potential danger.

**Reference Formula:** KE = ½ * mass * velocity²

**Methodology:**
1.  Use the projectile's mass (in kg) from the design.
2.  Estimate the projectile's velocity (in m/s) based on the sling material, taper, active length, and draw length. Use known performance data for comparable setups if possible.
3.  If critical data (like projectile mass or specific band details) is missing, state **"INSUFFICIENT DATA"** and specify which parameters are needed to complete the calculation.
