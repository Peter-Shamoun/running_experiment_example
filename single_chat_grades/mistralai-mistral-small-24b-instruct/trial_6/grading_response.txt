1.  **Calculate Draw Force (F):** Use the stress-strain formula F = E * A * (ΔL/L), where E is Young's Modulus, A is the total cross-sectional area, ΔL is the stretch distance, and L is the original length.
    *   E = 1.5 MPa = 1.5 x 10^6 N/m²
    *   A (for two bands) = 2 * (width * thickness) = 2 * (0.01 m * 0.002 m) = 0.00004 m²
    *   Strain (ΔL/L) = 0.1 m / 0.2 m = 0.5
    *   F = (1.5 x 10^6 N/m²) * (0.00004 m²) * (0.5) = **30 N** (This is ~6.7 lbs of draw force, which is low but plausible for the short draw.)

2.  **Estimate Stored Energy (KE):** Approximate the potential energy stored in the bands as KE ≈ 0.5 * F * ΔL.
    *   KE ≈ 0.5 * (30 N) * (0.1 m) = **1.5 Joules**
