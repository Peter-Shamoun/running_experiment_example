### **2. Band Attachment: The "Wrap and Tuck" Method**

This is a universally adopted method by high-performance enthusiasts because it's secure, self-tightening, and does not damage the bands.

**You will need:**
*   Your main bandset.
*   Two thin strips of latex, called "tie-in strips." These should be ~100 mm long and 8-10 mm wide, cut from a thinner latex sheet (e.g., 0.40mm).

**Procedure (for one fork tip):**

1.  **Position the Band:** Lay the wide end of your tapered band over the top of the fork, centered in the groove. Ensure about 15-20 mm of band material extends past the back of the fork. The band should lay flat and smooth.
2.  **Start the Wrap:** Place one end of the thin tie-in strip on top of the main band, holding it in place with your thumb.
3.  **Wrap Tightly:** Begin wrapping the tie-in strip around the main band *and the fork tip*, pulling it very tight with each wrap. The wraps should be neat and slightly overlapping. Work your way down the fork tip. Complete 4-5 full wraps.
4.  **The "Tuck":** On your last wrap, as you come around the fork, loop the end of the tie-in strip *underneath* the final wrap itself.
5.  **Cinch it Down:** Pull the end of the tucked strip very hard. The friction of the stretched latex will lock it in place, preventing the wrap from unraveling. The entire wrap assembly will tighten down on itself.
6.  **Finish:** Trim the excess from both the main band and the tie-in strip for a clean look. The attachment should be incredibly secure.

Repeat for the other side. The result is a smooth, even-pressure attachment that won't create stress points on your power bands.
