### **1. Frame Fabrication: Method and Key Considerations**

For a maximum-performance, one-off build like this, the fabrication method is key to achieving the required precision and finish.

**Recommended Method: Hybrid Waterjet + CNC Milling**

While you could create the entire frame on a CNC mill, a more efficient and cost-effective professional approach is a hybrid method:

1.  **Waterjet Cutting (Primary Blank):** Start by using an abrasive waterjet to cut the 2D profile of the frame from a 12mm thick plate of 7075-T6 aluminum.
    *   **Why?** Waterjet cutting is extremely fast and economical for cutting thick metal plates. It imparts no heat into the material (no risk of altering the T6 temper) and gets you 90% of the way there by creating the basic shape.
2.  **CNC Milling (Finishing and 3D Contouring):** Secure the waterjet-cut blank into a 3-axis (or 5-axis) CNC milling machine for all the critical finishing work.
    *   **Why?** CNC milling is essential for the precision and 3D shaping that the waterjet cannot do. It is the only way to achieve the required tolerances and surface finishes for a high-performance build.

**Key Machining Considerations:**

*   **Fork Grooves (Most Critical Feature):**
    *   **Tooling:** Use a **ball-end mill** of the appropriate diameter (e.g., 6mm) to machine the band grooves. This naturally creates a rounded, "U"-shaped channel that will not cut into the latex bands. A square-end mill would create sharp corners that would guarantee band failure.
    *   **Surface Finish:** The surface finish inside these grooves must be flawless. After milling, the grooves should be **hand-polished**. Start with 400-grit sandpaper, move to 800-grit, then finish with a metal polishing compound (like Flitz or Mother's Mag & Aluminum Polish) on a Dremel felt wheel. The goal is a mirror-smooth finish to maximize band life.

*   **Handle Contouring:**
    *   **Toolpaths:** The ergonomic curves of the handle are machined using **3D surfacing toolpaths** on the CNC. The machine will make many fine, overlapping passes to create a smooth, organic shape.

*   **Final Finishing:**
    *   After all machining is complete, the entire frame should be **bead blasted**. This removes machine marks and provides a uniform, non-glare, slightly textured matte finish that improves grip.
    *   For ultimate durability and corrosion resistance, follow bead blasting with **Type II anodizing**. This creates a hard, ceramic-like surface layer. A black or clear matte anodize would look exceptionally professional.
