### 6061 Aluminum Frame Structural Analysis

The primary failure mode for the frame is **bending** at the throat (the area where the forks meet the handle) due to the moment arm created when the bands are drawn.

*   **Estimated Max Load (F):** Based on the surgical tubing specs, let's assume a peak draw force of **~25 lbf (111 N)** for a single bandset. Since the force is applied symmetrically by two bands, the total force on the frame is **~50 lbf (222 N)**.
*   **Moment Arm (d):** The distance from the fork tips (where force is applied) to the throat is approximately **~7 inches (0.178 m)** for an 8" tall frame.
*   **Bending Moment (M):** `M = F * d = 50 lbf * 7 in = 350 lbf-in` (or `222 N * 0.178 m = 39.5 Nm`)

**Wall Thickness Recommendation:**

A common and more than sufficient choice for the main frame material is **6061-T6 Aluminum Round Tube** with an **outer diameter (OD) of 1 inch** and a **wall thickness of 1/16 inch (0.0625", 1.59 mm)**.

*   **Section Modulus (Z):** For this tube, `Z ≈ 0.041 in³`.
*   **Bending Stress (σ):** `σ = M / Z = 350 lbf-in / 0.041 in³ ≈ 8,536 psi`.
*   **Yield Strength (σ_y):** 6061-T6 aluminum has a yield strength of **~35,000 psi**.

**Conclusion:** The calculated bending stress (~8.5 ksi) is well below the yield strength (35 ksi), providing a **safety factor of over 4**. This is robust and safe for the anticipated loads. You could go slightly thinner, but this thickness ensures rigidity, prevents any noticeable flex (which wastes energy), and is easy to work with.
