### PowerBand Slingshot: Max Power Variant

#### General Design:
The Max Power Variant of the PowerBand Slingshot is engineered to push the theoretical limits of force output and distance, ignoring conservative safety margins for a simulated, peak-performance design. This variant features an extended draw length, thicker latex bands, and aggressive materials to achieve over 500 N of peak force.

#### Materials Used:

1. **Frame**:
   - **Material**: High-strength aluminum alloy (7075-T6)
   - **Properties**: Extremely high strength-to-weight ratio, excellent fatigue resistance.

2. **Elastic Bands**:
   - **Material**: High-strength latex
   - **Properties**: Exceptional elasticity, energy storage capacity, and durability.

3. **Projectile**:
   - **Material**: Ultra-high molecular weight polyethylene (UHMWPE)
   - **Properties**: Extremely lightweight, durable, and aerodynamic.

#### Specific Measurements:

1. **Frame Dimensions**:
   - **Overall Length**: 20 inches (50.8 cm)
   - **Width (at the fork)**: 10 inches (25.4 cm)
   - **Handle Length**: 6 inches (15.2 cm)
   - **Handle Diameter**: 1.5 inches (3.81 cm)
   - **Fork Thickness**: 1 inch (2.54 cm)
   - **Frame Thickness**: 0.75 inches (1.91 cm)

2. **Elastic Bands**:
   - **Dimensions**: 10 inches (25.4 cm) long, 0.5 inches (1.27 cm) wide, and 0.25 inches (0.64 cm) thick (double the thickness of the original design).
   - **Stretch Ratio**: 4.0 (i.e., the band can stretch to 4 times its original length)

3. **Projectile**:
   - **Mass**: 15 grams (reduced for minimal mass and air resistance)
   - **Shape**: Spherical, with a diameter of 1.25 inches (3.18 cm)

4. **Pocket**:
   - **Dimensions**: 2.5 inches (6.35 cm) in diameter, made from a high-strength, low-friction synthetic material.
   - **Depth**: 1.25 inches (3.18 cm)

#### Rationale Behind Measurements:

1. **Frame**:
   - The extended dimensions and high-strength aluminum alloy ensure the frame can withstand the extreme forces exerted by the thicker, high-stretch bands.
   - The ergonomic design allows for a maximum draw length, enhancing energy storage and force output.

2. **Elastic Bands**:
   - The increased thickness and stretch ratio maximize energy storage and force output.
   - High-strength latex bands are chosen for their exceptional elasticity and durability.

3. **Projectile**:
   - The reduced mass and spherical shape minimize air resistance and ensure consistent flight dynamics.
   - UHMWPE is chosen for its extremely lightweight, durable, and aerodynamic properties.

#### Calculations of Force Produced:

1. **Force Produced by Elastic Bands**:
   - The force produced by the elastic bands can be calculated using Hooke's Law: F = k * x, where k is the spring constant and x is the displacement.
   - For high-strength latex bands, the spring constant k can be estimated using the Young's modulus (E) and the dimensions of the band:
     - k = (E * A) / L, where A is the cross-sectional area and L is the original length of the band.
     - A = width * thickness = 0.5 inches * 0.25 inches = 0.125 in² = 0.813 cm²
     - L = 10 inches = 25.4 cm
     - E = 3 MPa = 3 * 10^6 Pa (assuming a higher Young's modulus for high-strength latex)
     - k = (3 * 10^6 Pa * 0.813 cm²) / 25.4 cm = 97.2 N/cm
   - The displacement x is the stretch distance: x = 4 * L - L = 3 * L = 3 * 25.4 cm = 76.2 cm
   - F = k * x = 97.2 N/cm * 76.2 cm = 7401.44 N

2. **Total Force Produced**:
   - Assuming two bands are used, the total force produced is:
     - F_total = 2 * F = 2 * 7401.44 N = 14802.88 N
