## Energy Storage in Elastic Bands

**Fundamental Equation:**
Elastic Potential Energy: **E = ½kx²**

Where:
- E = energy (Joules)
- k = spring constant (N/m)
- x = extension distance (meters)

## Important Physics Considerations

### 1. **Efficiency Losses**
Real systems lose energy to:
- Hysteresis in latex (~15-20% loss)
- Air resistance
- Friction at mounting points
- **Actual transferred energy ≈ 60-80% of stored energy**

### 2. **Material Stress Limits**
- Latex safe extension: 300-400% of relaxed length
- Beyond this: permanent deformation or failure

### 3. **Force-Extension Relationship**
Latex bands are **not perfectly linear**:
- Better model: E = ∫F(x)dx
- At high extensions, k increases (strain-stiffening)

## Experimental Measurement Method

To determine actual k-value:

1. Hang band vertically
2. Add known masses
3. Measure extension
4. Plot Force vs. Extension
5. Calculate k from slope (F = kx)
