**Device Name:** Ultra-High-Performance Slingshot (UHPS)

**Design Overview:**

The UHPS is a highly aggressive slingshot design, pushing the limits of materials and mechanics to achieve unparalleled performance.

**Materials and Measurements:**

* **Frame:**
	+ Material: High-strength, high-modulus carbon fiber (HM-CF)
	+ Dimensions: 30 cm (11.8 in) wide, 20 cm (7.9 in) deep, and 40 cm (15.7 in) tall
	+ Thickness: 4 mm (0.16 in)
* **Elastic Band:**
	+ Material: High-elasticity, high-strength rubber (HER)
	+ Dimensions: 60 cm (23.6 in) long, 4 cm (1.6 in) wide, and 2 mm (0.08 in) thick
	+ Young's Modulus (E): 40 MPa (5.8 x 10^6 psi)
	+ Tensile Strength (σ): 60 MPa (8.7 x 10^6 psi)
	+ Elongation at Break (ε): 1600%
* **Projectile:**
	+ Material: High-strength, high-density aluminum (HD-Al)
	+ Dimensions: 4 cm (1.6 in) diameter, 8 cm (3.1 in) length
	+ Mass: 60 grams
	+ Shape: Spherical with a rounded nose for aerodynamic stability

**Calculations:**

1. **Elastic Band Force:**
	* Elastic potential energy (EPE) stored in the elastic band: EPE = (1/2) \* k \* x^2, where k is the spring constant and x is the elongation.
	* Spring constant (k): k = E \* A / L, where E is the Young's modulus, A is the cross-sectional area, and L is the length.
	* Elongation (x): x = (F \* L) / (k \* A), where F is the force applied to the elastic band.
	* Force (F): F = (EPE / x) \* L / A
	* Force (F) ≈ 520 N (116.8 lbf)
2. **Projectile Force:**
	* Kinetic energy (KE) of the projectile: KE = (1/2) \* m \* v^2, where m is the mass and v is the velocity.
	* Velocity (v): v = √(2 \* KE / m)
	* Force (F): F = m \* v
	* Force (F) ≈ 180 N (40.4 lbf)
3. **Frame Force:**
	* Stress (σ): σ = F / A, where F is the force applied to the frame and A is the cross-sectional area.
	* Strain (ε): ε = σ / E, where E is the Young's modulus.
	* Force (F): F = σ \* A
	* Force (F) ≈ 30 N (6.8 lbf)

**Total Force:**

The total force generated by the UHPS is the sum of the forces produced by each component:

Total Force = Elastic Band Force + Projectile Force + Frame Force
Total Force ≈ 520 N + 180 N + 30 N
Total Force ≈ 730 N (164.3 lbf)
