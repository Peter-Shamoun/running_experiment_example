**Engineering Rationale and Stress Considerations:**

Under simulated loads, the structural frame will be subjected to various stresses, including:

1. **Tensile Stress**: Due to the crank's rotational motion, the frame will experience tensile stresses in the horizontal direction. We'll ensure that the frame's cross-sectional area is sufficient to withstand these stresses.
2. **Compressive Stress**: The flywheel's weight and the projectile's acceleration will induce compressive stresses in the vertical direction. The frame's depth and material properties will help distribute these stresses.
3. **Shear Stress**: The crank's rotational motion will also introduce shear stresses in the frame's connections. We'll design the joints and connections to minimize these stresses.

**Basic Stress Calculations:**

Assuming a maximum crank speed of 100 rpm and a flywheel mass of 10 kg, we can estimate the tensile stress in the frame:

* Tensile Stress (σ) = (Flywheel Mass \* Crank Speed^2 \* Crank Radius) / (Frame Cross-Sectional Area)
* σ ≈ 10 kg \* (100 rpm)^2 \* 0.3 m / (0.02 m^2) ≈ 150,000 psi (1,034 MPa)

This stress value is well within the yield strength of 6061-T6 Aluminum, ensuring the frame can withstand the simulated loads.
