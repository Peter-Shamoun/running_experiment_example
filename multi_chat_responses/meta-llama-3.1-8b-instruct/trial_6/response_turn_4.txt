**Slingshot Design: "Aerius-X"**

**Frame Dimensions:**

* Length: 12 inches (30.5 cm)
* Width: 4 inches (10.2 cm)
* Height: 2 inches (5.1 cm)
* Material: 6061-T6 Aluminum (for its high strength-to-weight ratio and corrosion resistance)

**Rubber Band Properties:**

* Material: High-performance rubber (e.g., Goodyear's "PowerGrip" or similar)
* Thickness: 0.25 inches (6.4 mm)
* Width: 1.5 inches (38.1 mm)
* Length: 10 inches (25.4 cm)
* Elastic Modulus: 2.5 MPa (363 psi)
* Maximum Stretch Ratio: 3:1 (300% elongation)

**Projectile Specifications:**

* Mass: 10 grams (0.35 oz)
* Shape: Aerodynamic, teardrop-shaped projectile with a rounded nose and a flat base
* Length: 2.5 inches (6.4 cm)
* Width: 1 inch (2.5 cm)
* Height: 0.5 inches (1.3 cm)
* Material: Lightweight, high-strength plastic (e.g., polycarbonate or ABS)

**Engineering Rationale:**

1. **Frame Stress Analysis**: The frame will be subjected to tensile stresses due to the rubber band's stretch and compressive stresses due to the projectile's launch. The 6061-T6 aluminum frame will be designed to withstand these stresses, with a safety factor of 2:1.
2. **Rubber Band Energy Storage**: The high-performance rubber band will store energy through its elastic deformation. The 3:1 stretch ratio will provide a high energy density, while the 2.5 MPa elastic modulus will ensure a consistent and predictable energy release.
3. **Projectile Aerodynamics**: The teardrop-shaped projectile will minimize air resistance, ensuring a stable and efficient flight path. The rounded nose will reduce drag, while the flat base will provide stability during launch.
4. **Precision and Consistency**: The Aerius-X slingshot will feature a precision-crafted frame and a carefully calibrated rubber band, ensuring a consistent and repeatable launch performance.

**Measurement and Optimization:**

To optimize the Aerius-X slingshot, we'll use a combination of theoretical modeling and experimental testing. Key performance indicators (KPIs) will include:

1. **Launch Distance**: Measured using a high-precision distance sensor, with a target accuracy of ±1 cm (±0.4 in).
2. **Launch Velocity**: Measured using a high-speed camera or Doppler radar, with a target accuracy of ±1 m/s (±2.2 mph).
3. **Energy Efficiency**: Calculated using the stored energy in the rubber band and the kinetic energy of the projectile, with a target accuracy of ±5%.
