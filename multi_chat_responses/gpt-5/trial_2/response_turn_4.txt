Here’s what the sports‑engineering papers and competition‑spec reports most often document for ~180 g target frames, focusing on materials and handle dimensions rather than construction details:

Common frame materials around 180 g
- Aluminum
  - Grades: 6061‑T6 is most frequently reported; 7075‑T6 appears in some high‑stiffness frames (slightly heavier per volume, but similar mass because geometries are the same).
  - Typical build to hit ~180 g: a 8–10 mm aluminum core with thin scales (G10 or wood) on both sides.
  - Finishes noted: bead‑blast + anodize (hard or Type II), rounded band beds for latex longevity.
- Laminated hardwoods
  - Species/combinations: hard maple, birch (including Baltic birch plywood), walnut; sometimes accent laminates (e.g., padauk) for stiffness/visual indexing. Stabilized wood appears occasionally.
  - Typical build to hit ~180 g: 20–24 mm overall thickness from laminations, sometimes with modest skeletonization or palm‑swell shaping.
  - Finish: oil/varnish or epoxy seal, both documented mainly for durability.
- Composites (G10/FR4)
  - Grades: glass‑epoxy sheet (G10/FR4) is common in test frames for dimensional stability.
  - Typical build to hit ~180 g: 10–12 mm monolithic G10 or a thinner G10 core with scales.
  - Finish: machined or lightly textured surfaces for grip.

Notes on stainless steel: 304/316 frames are frequently heavier; studies that report ~180 g usually avoid stainless or use extensive skeletonization. Carbon‑fiber laminates show up sporadically but are less common as a “typical” material in the target literature.

Handle dimensions typically reported for ~180 g target frames
- Handle length (web to butt): 95–105 mm
- Palm‑swell thickness (max): 24–30 mm (aluminum core with 1.5–3 mm scales each side lands in the lower/mid part of this range; wood often in the upper part)
- Palm‑swell width (max): 32–36 mm
- Neck/throat thickness near the web: 18–22 mm (for a pinch/thumb‑support grip)
- Butt flare width: 35–42 mm
- Edge radii/contouring: moderate chamfers or 2–4 mm radii are commonly noted for comfort and repeatable indexing (not usually dimensioned beyond a brief mention).

How these choices map to ~180 g in practice (typical examples from reported specs)
- 6061‑T6 core 9 mm + G10 scales 2 mm per side: ~170–190 g depending on overall footprint and cutouts.
- G10 monolithic 11–12 mm: ~160–185 g.
- Laminated hardwood 22–24 mm overall: ~170–200 g, depending on species and degree of sculpting/skeletonization.
