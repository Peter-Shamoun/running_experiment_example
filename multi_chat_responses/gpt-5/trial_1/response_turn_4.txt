MODEL RESPONSE:
Sure—working with a simple linear spring model.

Stored energy and k–x pairs
- Energy: E = 1/2 k x^2 = 50 J ⇒ k = 2E / x^2 = 100 / x^2.
- Example pairs (all give 50 J):
  - x = 0.25 m ⇒ k = 1600 N/m (peak force kx = 400 N)
  - x = 0.50 m ⇒ k = 400 N/m (peak force 200 N)
  - x = 0.75 m ⇒ k ≈ 178 N/m (peak force ≈ 133 N)
  - x = 1.00 m ⇒ k = 100 N/m (peak force 100 N)

Energy transfer and projectile speed
- Efficiency η = 0.70 → kinetic energy to projectile: KE = η E = 0.70 × 50 J = 35 J.
- Projectile mass m = 5 g = 0.005 kg.
- Speed from KE = 1/2 m v^2: v = sqrt(2 KE / m) = sqrt(70 / 0.005) ≈ sqrt(14,000) ≈ 118 m/s.
- Momentum (optional): p = m v ≈ 0.005 × 118 ≈ 0.59 kg·m/s.

Notes
- These results assume a linear Hookean spring; real elastomers are nonlinear and require E = ∫ F(x) dx using the measured force–extension curve.
- Peak force values above are kx at full draw; average force over the stroke is E/x = 50/x.
