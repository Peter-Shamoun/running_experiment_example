How frames are described

- Materials and band anchors:
  - CNC aluminum, stainless, laminated wood, and G10/FR4 are common; edges are radiused at the band bed to limit stress on latex.
  - Anchoring via tie‑in posts, slots, or clamping plates; authors note that anchor repeatability matters for test validity.

How band sets are described
- Material and topology:
  - Flat natural latex sheets are most common in target contexts; small‑bore latex tubing appears in some studies.
  - Configurations: single flat band, “double” (stacked) bands, and tapered bands (wider at the fork, narrower at the pouch) to balance draw force, speed, and stability.
- Dimensional descriptors:
  - Thickness for flat bands is sub‑millimetre; studies specify thickness and fork/pouch widths and whether a taper is used.
  - Taper is typically modest (roughly 20–40% reduction from fork end to pouch end).

- Mechanical descriptors and test conditions:
  - Elongation (stretch) ratio at full draw is usually in the ~4.5× to 5.5× range for competitive target ammo.
  - Draw force at anchor is typically reported as a peak value, often in the ~40–100 N (≈4–10 kgf) bracket for target setups.
  - Authors often report force–displacement curves, hysteresis, and temperature sensitivity (latex performance changes with temperature and cycle count).
  - Preconditioning cycles and ambient temperature (e.g., ~20–25 C) are documented for reproducibility.
- Life and consistency:
  - Cycle life and shot‑to‑shot velocity variation are noted; higher elongation improves speed but reduces band life and consistency.

How projectiles and performance are described
- Projectile classes:
  - Spherical steel shot dominates; 8.0 mm and 9.5 mm (3/8 in) diameters are the most commonly documented competition sizes.
  - Approximate masses (from density of steel): 8.0 mm ≈ 2.1 g; 9.5 mm ≈ 3.5 g; 10.0 mm ≈ 4.1 g.

- Kinematics and efficiency:
  - Muzzle velocity is measured by optical or acoustic chronographs; typical values for target setups are on the order of tens of m/s (commonly ~45–70 m/s depending on band set and ammo).

- Accuracy/targets:
  - Test distances commonly 10 m (sometimes 15 m); target sizes are standardized in specific rule sets and are described to give context to group size or hit probability data.

Common reporting conventions you’ll see

- Band cross‑section, thickness, taper ratio, active length, and pouch geometry.
- Environmental conditions (temperature, humidity), pre‑stretch/cycling protocol, and the number of shots per data point.
